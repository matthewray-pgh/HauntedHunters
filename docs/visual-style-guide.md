# Core Visual Identity

## What This World Looks Like

Haunted Hunters is not Gothic Victorian horror.
It is not haunted mansions, wrought iron gates, or top hats.

It is:

Appalachian rust belt decay interrupted by paranormal reality distortion.

The visual world is grounded, industrial, and American.
Horror enters through cracks in familiar, decayed infrastructure -
not through ornate architecture or fantasy aesthetics.

# Typography - Retained

**Primary (Titles, Headers):** Cinzel Decorative Bold
**Secondary (Body, Institutional):** Raleway Condensed

These fonts are kept. They read as serious and aged without being fantasy.
In document design, pair Cinzel headers with Raleway body text.

---

# Brand Mark - Retained

The HH monogram with compass/crosshair framing is kept as-is.
It reads as institutional and directional - appropriate for a containment organization.
It should appear worn and stamped, never clean or glossy.

# What Changes - The Imagery

## Out Completely

- Gothic Victorian mansions
- Ornate wrought iron gates
- Top hats and Victorian hunting coats
- European Gothic architecture
- Fantasy creature aesthetics
- Clean, polished environments

## In - The Haunted Hunters Visual World

### Primary Environment Types

- Collapsed mine shafts and underground tunnels
- Abandoned Appalachian industrial buildings
- Rust belt factory interiors - broken windows, collapsed ceilings
- Rural Pennsylvania farmland - decayed, isolated
- Underground research facilities - brutalist concrete, water damage
- Transit tunnels - fluorescent lighting, deteriorating signage
- Flooded subterranean spaces

### Character Aesthetic

- Practical field gear - canvas, leather, worn denim
- Analog equipment - EMF readers, reel-to-reel recorders, early radio equipment
- Gas masks, respirators, utility belts
- Lanterns (kept from brand sheet - functional, not decorative)
- Directorate field patches and worn insignia
- No fantasy armor. No Victorian coats.

### Lighting

- Single practical light sources - lanterns, flashlights, fluorescent flicker
- Green-teal ambient from industrial surroundings
- Amber from lantern and analog equipment glow
- Purple only in Veil-adjacent scenes
- High contrast - deep shadow, isolated light pools

### Atmosphere

- Heavy fog and particulate in air (mine dust, industrial vapor)
- Water damage - dripping, pooling, flooding
- Rust, corrosion, structural decay
- Overgrown vegetation reclaiming industrial structures
- Analog equipment - CRT monitors, reel-to-reel, radio equipment visible in scenes

# Image Usage Rules

## Consistency Rules

- Always use the master base prompt as foundation
- Never regenerate a canonical entity image - lock it on first approval
- Apply identical color grade in DaVinci Resolve to every image (teal shadows, amber highlights)
- Desaturate all images by 15-20% before use - keeps them feeling archival

## Overlay Treatment in DaVinci Resolve

Every AI image used in video should have:

- Film grain overlay (subtle)
- Slight vignette
- Color grade matching video palette
- Optional: scan line overlay for surveillance aesthetic

## Entity Visibility Rule

- Common entities: partially visible, environmental context dominant
- Rare entities: silhouette or environmental suggestion only
- Epic entities: single defining feature visible, rest in shadow
- Legendary entities: scale implied, full form never clear
- The First Phantom: never depicted. Ever.

## What Is Never Generated

- The First Phantom in any form
- Clear full-body entity reveals
- Victorian or Gothic architecture
- Fantasy weapons or armor
- Clean, modern, or bright environments
- Real locations or identifiable landmarks
