# Channel Identity

## Channel Name
Haunted Hunters

## Tagline
*"They didn't want this found."*

## Core Promise to the Viewer
Every video reveals something the Hunters Directorate has actively suppressed.
The channel exists because someone on the inside is leaking it.

---

# The Premise

The Haunted Hunters YouTube channel presents itself as a repository of leaked, recovered, and declassified Directorate materials — assembled and released by an anonymous insider known only as **THE ARCHIVIST**.

The Archivist does not appear on camera.
The Archivist does not explain themselves.
The Archivist releases documents, recordings, and evidence without commentary — except for sparse, unsigned annotations.

Viewers are not watching a lore channel.
They are watching a leak.

---

# The Archivist — Persona Rules

## Who They Are
Unknown. The channel never confirms identity, motivation, or clearance level.

## What They Do
- Release classified Directorate documents
- Annotate files with brief, unsettling observations
- Occasionally redact things — even within leaked materials
- Never break the fourth wall

## Voice Rules
- Never speaks directly to the audience
- Never uses "I" in annotations
- Annotations are sparse — one or two lines maximum
- Tone is clinical, never dramatic
- Urgency is implied, never stated

## Example Archivist Annotation Style
> *"File recovered from Ashfall Site C. Cross-reference with personnel record 7741. Notice what's missing."*

> *"This recording was scheduled for destruction. It was not destroyed."*

> *"Do not share this file."*

# Channel Voice & Tone

## What This Channel IS
- Institutional and cold
- Detail-obsessed
- Rewards careful viewers
- Treats the audience as intelligent
- Builds dread through implication, not declaration

## What This Channel IS NOT
- Sensationalist ("YOU WON'T BELIEVE THIS")
- Jumpscare or shock-driven
- Comedic or ironic
- Rushed or breathless
- Explained — the channel never over-explains

## The Golden Rule
> *What is left unsaid is more frightening than what is said.*

# Visual Identity

## Aesthetic
Analog horror. Institutional decay. Cold bureaucratic presentation interrupted by wrongness.

## Core Visual Elements
- VHS scan lines and signal degradation
- Typewriter and dot-matrix fonts for documents
- Amber and green CRT monitor tones
- Redacted black bars over text
- Directorate document headers and classification stamps
- Aged paper textures
- Deteriorating photography

## Color Palette
- Deep black
- Sickly amber
- Faded institutional green
- Bruised purple (Veil-adjacent content only)
- Blood red (Crimson-level incidents only)

## Logo Treatment
Stamped. Worn. Never clean. The channel logo should look like it was photocopied twelve times.

## Thumbnail Strategy
- Document-style layouts — classification stamps, redacted text, single unsettling image
- Never clickbait faces or exaggerated expressions
- Titles burned onto the thumbnail like file labels
- One visual detail that rewards close inspection

---

# Audio Identity

## Soundtrack Approach
- Ambient industrial drone
- Analog static and interference
- Distant mechanical sounds
- No melodic music unless diegetic (radio, intercom)

## Voice Treatment (when narration is used)
- Slightly processed — subtle tape hiss or room tone
- Never studio-clean
- Paced slowly — let silence breathe
- No music under narration

## Sound Design Priorities
- Document page turns
- Reel-to-reel tape movement
- Radio static
- Distant, ambiguous sounds beneath primary audio

# Content Pillars

Every video falls into one of these content types.

## Pillar 1 — Incident Reports
Breakdown of a specific historical Directorate incident.
*Format: Document reading + environmental footage + Archivist annotation*
*Example: The Black Vein Collapse — 1968*

## Pillar 2 — Entity Files
Classified entity dossier presented as a leaked Directorate record.
*Format: Classification document + recovered footage or field audio*
*Example: HH-013 — The Conductor*

## Pillar 3 — Recovered Logs
Audio or video recordings recovered from failed operations.
*Format: Found footage / audio log with sparse on-screen text*
*Example: Echo-4 Team — Station 13 Final Recordings*

## Pillar 4 — Directorate Intelligence
Internal Directorate documents — memos, personnel files, faction communications.
*Format: Document reading with redactions visible*
*Example: Internal Memo — The Threshold Initiative*

## Pillar 5 — The First Phantom Thread
An ongoing ARG-adjacent mystery seeded across multiple videos.
Fragments of The First Phantom's existence appear in the background of other content.
No video is ever explicitly about The First Phantom — it simply accumulates.
*This is the long game.*

# The ARG Layer — Rules

The First Phantom thread operates as a passive ARG woven through the channel.

## How It Works
- Each video contains one subtle reference to The First Phantom
- References are never highlighted or explained
- They accumulate across videos into a larger picture
- The community discovers and documents them organically

## Examples of Hidden References
- A redacted name in a document that is always the same length
- A specific static pattern that appears briefly in multiple videos
- A personnel number that appears in unrelated incident reports
- A sound frequency present under multiple recordings

## Rules
- Never confirm ARG elements in comments
- Never deny them either
- Let the community build the mythology
- Pay it off eventually — but not soon

---

# Content Rules — Canon Compliance

Every video must:
- Reinforce at least one creative pillar (industrial decay, isolation, subterranean tension, institutional secrecy, environmental corruption, analog limitation, Veil instability)
- Be consistent with established canon
- Leave at least one question unanswered
- Contain at least one detail that rewards a second viewing

Every video must NOT:
- Fully explain any major mystery
- Show the Veil directly or completely
- Confirm The First Phantom's nature
- Break the institutional voice with humor or irony
- Use clean, modern visual aesthetics

# Community Strategy

## Comment Approach
The Archivist does not respond to comments.
The channel account may occasionally pin a comment — always a cryptic document fragment, never a direct answer.

## Community Posts
Used sparingly. Format: redacted document excerpts, classification stamps, single-line annotations.

## Engagement Philosophy
Reward the attentive. Ignore the impatient.
The audience that stays is the audience worth keeping.

# Monetization Alignment

## YouTube Monetization
- Video length targets: 8–15 minutes (ad break eligible, maintains tension)
- Consistent uploads trigger algorithm favor even on irregular schedules if watch time is high
- Lore channels with ARG elements generate exceptional watch time — viewers rewatch to find details

## Future Monetization Paths
- Directorate document print bundles (merch)
- Patreon — "Clearance Level" tiers with early access to files
- Game early access integration when ready
- Licensing lore to companion podcast or audio drama
