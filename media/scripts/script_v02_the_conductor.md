---

# Script Notes

## Runtime Target
6–8 minutes | ~700–900 words of narration

## Voice Direction
Same Archivist treatment as Video 01 — ElevenLabs, tape recorder playback,
slight wow and flutter, subtle hiss, narrow frequency range.
Not distorted. Not robotic. Worn.
This is the first Entity File. The voice should sound slightly more careful here —
like someone reading a document they've read before and don't enjoy rereading.

## Visual Approach
Two visual systems in this video, kept deliberately distinct:

- **[DOCUMENT]** — the HH-013 classification file itself. Same treatment as
  Video 01's orientation document. Typewriter font, aged paper, CRT overlay.
- **[SURVEILLANCE INSERT]** — recovered VHS security footage. Distinct from
  the document system entirely. Tracking error glitch-cut in and out
  (never a dissolve — this is recovered footage, not a document photograph).
  Timestamp burn-in, bottom left, degraded characters. Persistent tracking
  jitter across the full frame while active.

**[FULL FRAME]** used once — maximum impact moment only, per house style.

## On-Screen Text
No on-screen Archivist text during narration. Closing annotation card only.
Timestamp burn-in on surveillance inserts is diegetic, not Archivist text —
it belongs to the footage, not to the narrator.

## Silence Note
[BEAT] — minimum two seconds.
[LONG BEAT] — minimum four seconds.
Performance cues, not editing mistakes. Same as Video 01.

## Image Transition Note
Document visuals: slow dissolves, minimum four seconds hold, matching Video 01.
Surveillance inserts: hard glitch-cut in, tracking jitter throughout, glitch-cut out.
The two systems should never transition into each other smoothly —
the seam between "document" and "recovered footage" should always be felt.

**SFX — DISTORTED PA ANNOUNCEMENT (diegetic, no narration under this)**

A transit announcement plays — heavily degraded, pitched slightly wrong,
words dragging into each other. It should be *almost* intelligible.
The audience should feel like they nearly caught what it said.
Recommended fragment: a departure announcement that trails into silence
mid-sentence, followed by two seconds of dead air, followed by the sound
of a train approaching that never arrives.

no narration.
