# How to Read This Document

Each scene block contains:
- **SCENE** — scene number and name
- **TIMECODE** — approximate start time (adjust as your actual pacing dictates)
- **DURATION** — how long this scene runs
- **VISUAL** — what is on screen and how the camera/image moves
- **AUDIO** — what is heard, at what level
- **CAPCUT NOTES** — specific layer or effect instructions
- **NARRATION** — exact words spoken in this scene (if any)

---

# CapCut Layer Stack Reference

This video runs three visual systems: the document (Video 01/02
baseline), the location insert (excavation map), and the testimony
card — new for this video, and the quietest system on the channel so far.

```
Layer 6 (TOP)   — Text overlays (pre-roll text, annotation card, testimony cards)
Layer 5         — (unused this video — no VHS tracking system here)
Layer 4         — CRT scan line overlay (document + full frame scenes only, Screen blend, 20%)
Layer 3         — Archival grain / film damage overlay (Screen, 10% opacity)
Layer 2         — Images (document, location insert, full frame)
Layer 1 (BASE)  — Black background (solid color, full duration)

Audio Track 1   — Narration (-6db)
Audio Track 2   — Mining ambient (-18db, drops to -24db during testimony cards)
Audio Track 3   — Electrical hum (-24db)
Audio Track 4   — Page turn / incidental sounds (-12db)
Audio Track 5   — Testimony card cut sound (scene-specific, see Scenes 09, 10, 11)
```

**Rule:** This video does NOT use the VHS tracking jitter system from
Video 02. Testimony cards use a hard cut, not a glitch-cut — the
distinction matters. A glitch-cut says "damaged recording." A hard cut
says "a different document was placed in front of you." Keep those
two transition languages separate across the channel.

# RUNTIME NOTE

This scene-by-scene scaffold totals approximately **4:54**, under the
7–8 minute target — the same gap pattern as Videos 01 and 02's
storyboard scaffolds before BEAT tuning in the edit.

To reach 7–8 minutes, extend primarily by:
- Lengthening [BEAT] and [LONG BEAT] holds in Scenes 06, 08, 13, and 15
  — these carry the densest narration-free space and are the safest
  place to add air
- Slowing the Ken Burns pan rate across Scenes 05–16 so the camera
  covers more distance in the same reading time
- Confirming Scenes 10, 11, and 12 (testimony cards) all hit their
  full minimum holds — these three plus Scene 14's full frame account
  for nearly a full minute and are not candidates for trimming

Do not pad by adding a fourth testimony card or a second full-frame
break — both are locked to house style (three cards, one full frame
maximum). Runtime is a pacing problem, not a content problem.
