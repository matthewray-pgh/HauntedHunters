# The Hunters System

## Status
Core Gameplay System

---

# Overview

Hunters are collectible playable characters within Haunted Hunters.

Each Hunter represents a specialized role in paranormal investigation and containment.

Hunters are not just player avatars — they function as:
- gameplay loadouts
- ability kits
- progression targets
- collectible units
- and strategic tools for missions

---

# Core Design Philosophy

Instead of focusing solely on weapons or equipment, players build a roster of Hunters.

Each Hunter provides:
- unique abilities
- situational advantages
- specialization roles
- and progression incentives

This creates a long-term collection and mastery loop.

---

# Hunter Roles

## Investigator
Balanced generalist role.

### Ability: Reveal Weakness
- Highlights entity vulnerabilities
- Increases capture probability
- Improves investigation efficiency

---

## Exorcist
Specialist in spiritual entities.

### Ability: Holy Seal
- Weakens ghost-type entities
- Temporarily suppresses manifestations
- Improves survival in spirit-heavy zones

---

## Mechanic
Specialist in technological systems and anomalies.

### Ability: Electro Trap
- Deploys electrical traps
- Stuns mechanical or system-based horrors
- Disrupts entity-linked electronics

---

## Cryptozoologist
Specialist in creature-based entities.

### Ability: Calm Beast
- Reduces aggression of animalistic anomalies
- Increases rare encounter probability
- Improves capture stability

---

## Medium
Specialist in spirit communication.

### Ability: Spirit Sense
- Detects hidden or invisible entities
- Reveals concealed environmental threats
- Tracks paranormal activity through intuition

---

# Gameplay Role Function

Hunters are used to:
- modify mission strategy
- counter specific entity types
- unlock hidden interactions
- improve capture efficiency
- and influence encounter outcomes

---

# Progression System

Hunters can be:
- leveled
- upgraded
- specialized
- and customized

---

# Strategic Layer

Players must choose Hunters carefully before missions based on:
- expected entity type
- environmental conditions
- risk level
- and mission objectives
